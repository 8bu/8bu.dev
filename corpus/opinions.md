I try not to pick fights over frameworks. The stack should serve the product, not the other way around. That said, I do have positions that come from where I've actually shipped.

## Vue vs React

I lean Vue - it's where most of my experience is, so I reach for it by default. But I've delivered production React as well, and I reach for it when the job calls for it. Whichever fits the team and the problem. Vue is my home turf; I've shipped plenty of React too. I don't pick fights over it.

## Vue depth

Vue is genuinely my strength. Most of my history is Vue - Vue 2 and Vue 3, Nuxt 2 through 4. I'm primarily a Vue developer, with React/Next alongside.

## TypeScript

TypeScript-first. It's my primary language. Not really a debate for me.

## Tailwind vs SCSS

I've shipped both in production - Tailwind across WegoPro, Multiplier, and SuperLauncher, SCSS too when it fits. Tailwind for speed, SCSS when the design system needs the deeper work. It's not a holy war.

## Testing

Pragmatic, not dogmatic. I've used Jest, Cypress, and Vitest in production. I test what's worth protecting rather than chasing a coverage target. Whether tests come first or after depends on the work in front of me.
