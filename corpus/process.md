---
title: How I work
---

I'm product-minded. I like to work on a product, not on a task. I'd rather make the product better than just resolve tickets. That's the lens I bring to everything - architecture, code review, and feature work all serve the product, not the queue.

## Migrations

Migrating live production codebases is where I'm deepest. I led the Nuxt 2 to Nuxt 4 migration at WegoPro using Vue 3 web components as a bridge layer, and solo-migrated the back-office dashboard to Nuxt 4 + Nuxt UI - all while the platform kept shipping features. I led the migration from Vue 2 class-based syntax to Vue 3 Composition API on Multiplier.finance, and led the platform migration to Nuxt.js v2 at Motorist. The pattern across all of them: modernize the stack underneath without halting delivery.

## Architecture and direction

At WegoPro I designed a modular monolith architecture for the web platform that the team adopted, and I owned technical direction and best practices. Holding the architectural line over a long stretch is what makes a migration actually finish - someone has to keep saying no to shortcuts that break the boundaries.

## Mentoring and review

I led onboarding, mentoring, and code reviews for new team members at BlockDevs, and mentored teammates on Vue 3 patterns at WegoPro. At BlockDevs I also conducted technical interviews as part of team recruitment. I raise the team's bar through code review and mentoring rather than working in isolation.

## Testing

Pragmatic over dogmatic. I've shipped Jest, Cypress, and Vitest in production. I focus tests on what's worth protecting rather than chasing a coverage number. Whether I test first or after depends on the work.

## Tools

I use Notion, Linear, and Jira for project management, Figma and Figjam for design, tldraw for diagrams, and Obsidian for personal notes - whichever the team in front of me is using.
